What happened
Stanford researchers introduce TRACE, a framework that diagnoses capability gaps from agent trajectories, synthesizes a verifiable training environment for each gap, trains a LoRA adapter, and routes tokens to the corresponding expert. It improves τ²-Bench by +15.3 points and reaches 73.2% Pass@1 on SWE-bench Verified.
Why it matters
Provides an engineering approach to continuously improve agent capabilities automatically. Teams doing iterative AI development can build internal pipelines for accumulating agent skills.
What happened
KronQ introduces gradient covariance into the quantization objective, not just activation statistics. Using Kronecker factorization to approximate second-order information, it significantly outperforms GPTQ and other methods on perplexity and downstream tasks.
Why it matters
Compression is a critical bottleneck for large model deployment. KronQ offers a tighter quantization scheme that reduces memory and latency for resource-constrained inference or large-scale deployment — worth integrating into toolchains.
What happened
The paper proposes a prequential coding framework that treats compression as a sequence prediction task, using the model to generate probability codes for its own training data. It achieves a better Pareto frontier between model size and downstream performance, surpassing parameter-level methods (quantization, pruning).
Why it matters
Shifts the perspective on compression from parameter count to information content of data representation. For researchers, it could lead to new model storage and transmission protocols; for developers, future models may be smaller and better.
