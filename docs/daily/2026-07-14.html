📄 Papers

What happened
This paper proposes a novel weak-to-strong generalization paradigm: run RL with verifiable rewards on a small model (cost-efficient), then distill the post-training knowledge to a strong model. Compared to directly running RL on the strong model, this approach dramatically reduces compute cost while still delivering significant reasoning improvements on the strong model.
Why it matters
RLVR training is becoming a post-training performance bottleneck (requiring many rollouts). This paper offers a practical path: explore with small models, transfer with distillation — potentially changing the economics and efficiency of future large model fine-tuning.
Connections
Today Rich Sutton founded Oak Lab to focus on true RL agents, complementing this paper's approach — one optimizes RL efficiency, the other returns to RL philosophy.
What happened
This survey systematically reviews the definition, measurement, and progress of metacognitive abilities in LLMs. It argues that metacognition (awareness of one's own cognitive processes) is foundational for building transparent, reliable AI systems, and identifies current LLM shortcomings: lack of reliable perception of knowledge boundaries, weak strategy selection, and susceptibility to prompting.
Why it matters
Metacognition is key to improving agent reliability — when to admit uncertainty, when to ask for help, how to select strategies. The survey provides a clear roadmap and suggests future post-training may need to incorporate metacognitive training signals.
What happened
This paper proposes KronQ, a new post-training quantization framework that introduces gradient covariance (via Kronecker factorization) to guide quantization, challenging traditional methods that assume equal importance for all output channels. On multiple LLMs, KronQ outperforms GPTQ and current SOTA at 4-bit and 3-bit settings.
Why it matters
Quantization is a key technology for deploying large models. KronQ has a more rigorous second-order optimization foundation and experiments show it maintains quality even at extreme low-bit settings, valuable for edge deployment and inference optimization.
What happened
This paper introduces a test-time training (TTT) method without external labels, where the LLM uses its own generated labels for instance-specific parameter updates during inference, significantly improving evidence localization and utilization in long-context tasks. Performance improved by over 10% on multiple long-context benchmarks.
Why it matters
Long-context utilization is a recognized weakness — extending the context window doesn't guarantee effective use. This paper offers a practical TTT solution that can dynamically improve inference quality on long inputs without modifying model weights.
