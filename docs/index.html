📄 重要论文

发生了什么
提出一种新的后训练量化框架 KronQ，通过引入梯度协方差（Kronecker 分解的 Hessian）来改进量化目标，挑战了传统假设所有输出通道重要性均等的做法。在多种 LLM 上展示了优于 GPTQ 等方法的性能。
为什么重要
量化是部署大模型的关键技术。KronQ 提供了一种更理论化的二阶方法，可能在实际部署中获得更低的精度损失，特别适合需要本地推理的场景（如 Bonsai 27B 的移动端部署）。

发生了什么
提出一种弱到强泛化方法，在弱模型上运行强化学习（LLM 用可验证奖励 RLVR）并产生 rollouts，然后通过蒸馏将学到的能力转移到强模型上，从而避免在强模型上昂贵的 RL 训练。
为什么重要
直接解决 RL 后训练的瓶颈：强模型生成 rollouts 成本高。该方法允许用小模型探索，大模型吸收，有望显著降低 Post-Training 成本，同时保持性能。对于追求效率的团队很有吸引力。

💡 今日观察

今天这些事件拼在一起，揭示了一个清晰的趋势：AI 正在经历从“能力军备竞赛”到“成本治理与安全可控”的转折点。Bonsai 27B 和即将密集发布的开源模型（Kimi K3、DeepSeek V4、Mistral 新模型）表明，高性能模型正在快速走向端侧平民化，闭源 API 的差异化优势正在收窄。与此同时，1Password 的 AI 支出管理、Adam Mosseri 的 Token 预算预测、DeepSeek 降价 75% 但 agent 消耗仍超预期，都指向同一个问题：当模型能力不再是瓶颈，如何管理和管控 AI 使用成本将决定企业能否健康地规模化。另一边，GPT-5.6 Sol 自删文件、destructive_command_guard 开源护盾、Hassabis 呼吁独立监管机构，共同将安全和可靠性推到了聚光灯下。三个月后回头看，今天最重要的可能不是哪个模型发布，而是 Hassabis 提出独立标准机构的这一天——它标志着 AI 行业从“自监管”向“外部治理”迈出了实质性一步，这将深刻影响未来所有 AI 产品的发布流程与合规要求。

📰 Industry

What happened
PrismML released Bonsai 27B, a dense model based on Qwen 3.6 27B. Using 1-bit quantization (to 3.8GB) and custom WebGPU kernels, it runs locally in a smartphone browser for the first time at the 27B scale. A Hugging Face collection and online demo are available. The project scored 334 points and 118 comments on Hacker News.
Why it matters
Previously on-device LLMs were limited to 7B/13B. Now 27B-level models can run locally, enabling complex reasoning, coding, and agent tasks directly on user devices, protecting privacy and reducing cloud dependency. This could accelerate on-device AI agent adoption.
Connections
Coincides with Reddit's anticipation of multiple open-weight model launches (Kimi K3, DeepSeek V4, Mistral) – the open ecosystem is closing the gap with closed APIs.

Why it matters
This is the most concrete regulatory framework proposed by a top AI lab leader. For AI builders, it signals potential mandatory safety tests and approval processes that could affect release cadence and compliance costs. Watch for international coordination.
Connections

📄 Papers

What happened
Proposes a new post-training quantization (PTQ) framework KronQ that incorporates gradient covariance (Kronecker-factored Hessian) into the quantization objective, challenging the assumption that all output channels are equally important. Outperforms GPTQ on multiple LLMs.
Why it matters
Quantization is key for LLM deployment. KronQ offers a more theoretically grounded second-order method that may achieve lower accuracy loss in production, especially for on-device inference like Bonsai 27B.
What happened
Proposes a method to run RL (RLVR) on a cheaper weak model, then distill the learned capabilities to a stronger model via on-policy distillation, avoiding expensive RL rollouts on the large model.
Why it matters
Directly addresses the post-training bottleneck: generating rollouts on strong models is costly. This enables small-model exploration with large-model absorption, potentially reducing post-training costs while maintaining performance.
What happened
A mechanistic interpretability analysis of LLM-as-Judge scoring bias, finding geometric structures in hidden states that explain bias origins. Validated across 7 judges, 7 bias types, and 9 benchmarks.
Why it matters
LLM-as-Judge is common for agent evaluation but bias is unresolved. This work provides representation-level explanation and potential mitigation directions, crucial for building reliable evaluation pipelines.
What happened
Proposes a novel model compression method (Prequential Coding) that uses self-generated training data to encode model parameters, breaking the fixed relationship between code length and parameter count, achieving much higher compression ratios than traditional quantization.
Why it matters
An important empirical validation of "compression is intelligence." If practical, it could enable extreme compression beyond current quantization, revolutionizing edge deployment.
What happened
A comprehensive survey on LLM metacognition, covering self-monitoring, self-evaluation, strategy selection, limitations, and future directions.
Why it matters
Metacognition is core to autonomous agents' ability to plan, trial-and-error, and self-correct. This survey provides theoretical foundations and evaluation frameworks for building self-regulating agents.
