**📄 Multi-Agent LLMs Fail to Explore Each Other**
- **What happened**: The paper formalizes multi-agent collaboration as a partially observable stochastic game (POSG) and finds that state-of-the-art LLM agents (including GPT-5.6, Claude Fable) exhibit myopic and polarized behavior patterns when interacting with other agents. - **Why it matters**: Multi-agent systems need more attention to exploration mechanisms before deployment. If you use multiple LLM agents collaborating on multi-step workflows, this paper reveals a real risk: agents may converge to local optima and fail to adapt to environmental changes.

**📄 Weak-to-Strong Generalization via Direct On-Policy Distillation**
- **What happened**: The paper proposes a weak-to-strong approach: first running RL training on a smaller model (cheap), then distilling the trained policy via on-policy distillation to a larger target model. Experiments on reasoning tasks show this approach saves significant compute compared to running RL directly on the large model, with near-par performance. Key: using the small model's outputs to supervise the large model, but the large model gets to see its own rollouts.
- **Why it matters**: For resource-constrained teams, this means leveraging small model RL training to improve large model capabilities. It's a practical post-training strategy that reduces fine-tuning costs for reasoning models.
- Importance: ★★★★★
- Source: [HuggingFace Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.05394)
