**📄 Scaling Laws for Hypernetwork-Based Knowledge Injection in Large Language Models**
- **What happened**: This paper systematically studies using hypernetworks to generate LoRA adapters for injecting knowledge into LLMs. The hypernetwork is trained on a large corpus of facts to generate a LoRA for each fact; inserting it enables the model to answer related questions. - **Why it matters**: Provides theoretical foundation and scaling formulas for dynamic, controllable knowledge injection. For AI builders needing frequent knowledge updates (enterprise knowledge bases, medical/legal specialized models), this is a predictable, cost-calculable alternative.

**📄 Beyond Euclidean Clipping: Overcoming Exploration Collapse in LLM RL via Riemannian Isometric Policy Optimization**
- **What happened**: This paper for the first time theoretically reveals the root cause of PPO-Clip failure in LLM RL: it implicitly measures policy discrepancy using Euclidean metric, inconsistent with the intrinsic Riemannian geometry of the policy manifold, causing exploration collapse. The authors propose Riemannian Isometric Policy Optimization (RIPO), maintaining isometric updates on the policy manifold, significantly outperforming PPO-Clip on math reasoning tasks.
- **Why it matters**: Most LLM fine-tuning uses PPO variants; exploration collapse is a well-known bottleneck. RIPO offers a theoretically elegant and practically effective alternative. A must-read for researchers in RL-driven LLM (RLHF, reasoning enhancement).
- **Importance**: ★★★★★
- **Source**: [HuggingFace Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.10169)

**📄 SoftReason: A Fully Differentiable Neuro-Soft-Symbolic Deductive Reasoning Architecture**
- **What happened**: The paper proposes a fully differentiable neuro-soft-symbolic deductive reasoning architecture. It infers latent facts from high-dimensional perceptual inputs, then performs differentiable deductive reasoning via soft Euler/graph traversal, using predicate vocabulary and rule definitions from a knowledge graph. The entire chain is end-to-end differentiable, trainable via backpropagation.
- **Why it matters**: Existing neuro-symbolic systems often have discrete interfaces between perception and reasoning, causing training difficulties. SoftReason enables end-to-end differentiable deductive reasoning, providing a new foundational framework for tasks needing perception + rule reasoning (visual QA, scene understanding, document reasoning).
- **Importance**: ★★★★★
- **Source**: [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20402v1)
