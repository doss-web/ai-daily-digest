## 📄 Papers

**📄 PUST: A Modular LLM Post-Training Paradigm via Proxy-Guided Update Signals**
- **What happened**: This paper proposes PUST (Proxy-guided Update Signal Transfer), which decouples policy exploration from distribution alignment in post-training. It enables cheap exploration on a small proxy model, then transfers optimization signals (reward models, etc.) to a larger target model. Experiments show PUST achieves performance comparable to full RLHF at a fraction of the cost.
- **Why it matters**: Current RLHF/RLVR requires expensive rollout on every new model. PUST's "train once, transfer many" approach, if scalable, could dramatically reduce the cost of continuous post-training. Especially important for research teams and companies that frequently update models.
- Importance: ★★★★★
- Source: [HuggingFace Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.11505)

**📄 KronQ: LLM Quantization via Kronecker-Factored Hessian**
- **What happened**: KronQ is a post-training quantization framework that introduces Kronecker approximation of the gradient covariance matrix to build more accurate quantization objectives. Unlike second-order methods like GPTQ that only use activation statistics, KronQ accounts for differing importance across output channels. KronQ outperforms existing methods in perplexity and downstream tasks across multiple LLMs and bit settings.
- **Why it matters**: Quantization is critical for deploying large models. KronQ offers a computationally affordable method with significant gains, potentially replacing GPTQ/AWQ. Teams needing to deploy 70B+ models on limited hardware should evaluate it.
- Importance: ★★★★★
- Source: [HuggingFace Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.07964)

**📄 Weak-to-Strong Generalization via Direct On-Policy Distillation**
- **What happened**: This research explores a weak-to-strong post-training method: run RLVR on a lightweight model, then distill the learned knowledge to a stronger target model. Experiments show that with carefully designed on-policy distillation, the weak model's post-RL output can effectively improve the strong model's reasoning capabilities at far lower cost than running RL directly on the strong model.
- **Why it matters**: It directly challenges the "bigger is better" RL paradigm. If weak-to-strong generalization becomes reliable, post-training compute costs could be reduced several-fold, especially benefiting smaller teams—they can explore with smaller models and then empower larger ones.
- Importance: ★★★★★
- Source: [HuggingFace Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.05394)
